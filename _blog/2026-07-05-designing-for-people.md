One idea has stayed with me throughout my research: **technology should adapt
to people, not the other way around.**

It sounds obvious, but most interfaces still ask users to learn their logic —
their menus, their vocabulary, their assumptions about who the "average" user
is. For older adults, for people with disabilities, and for anyone under
pressure, that gap becomes a wall.

## What AI changes

Large language models shift what's possible here. A system can now:

- Meet people in *their* words instead of a fixed command set
- Infer context and fill in what the user didn't say
- Explain itself when something goes wrong

> The goal isn't to make AI more impressive. It's to make the technology
> disappear, so the task is all that's left.

That's the lens I bring to everything I build now — and it's what I'll keep
writing about here.
